This article argues that AI output quality for lawyers depends on the quality of the prompt, the source material, and the lawyer's review, contrasting vague prompts with structured guidance. It introduces the CLAIM framework (Context, Legal task, Audience, Instructions, Mode of output) and provides ready-to-use templates for litigation, transactional, and in-house legal teams.
